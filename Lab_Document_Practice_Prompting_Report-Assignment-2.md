# Lab Document Practice — Prompting Report (Assignment 2)

## Novice vs Power User

- The prompt should be detailed enough so the writer gets the required answer instead of a vague and generic one.
- There must be context to the prompt so the AI can understand your requirements.
- Give AI room to give you some options. In this way you can avoid AI hallucinations.

## Knows vs Guesses

- To get the correct figures and details, mention the exact citation to fetch the data, or ask AI to "clearly mention if you don't know the answer."
- After getting the answer, ask about the citation or references.

## The 3 Retrieval Modes

- Evaluate your questions before asking. Design your questions around whether you want AI to use Web Search, answer from memory, or do deep research. Your words instruct AI to use one of those three modes.

## Context is Everything

- Give clear instructions to get desired results.
- Where needed, give pointers.
- Mention your audience, i.e., team, students, mid-level employees.

## Think Hard

- Use the phrase "Think Hard" in your prompt if you feel the answer must have deeper details.

## Stop the Flattery

- Don't force your perspective.
- Use the words "Compare, Find, Prove, Defend, Confirm, or Evaluate" to get a neutral or better answer.

## The Brainstorm – Iterate Loop

- After the first response, ask AI to explain or brief one of the key points.
- Ask AI to give 5 different versions of the given response.
- Ask for feedback/ideas on your given scenario or question.
- Pick your favorite option and ask AI to explain it a bit further.
- This way, you can reduce redundancies and/or hallucinations.
- Load context, ask for options, give feedback, repeat, expand.
- The value is not in the first answer — it's in the loop.

## Multimodal – Image & Audio

- You can ask AI to write a prompt for a specific image, audio, or video.
- While asking it to write a prompt, be objective. Visualize the result you want before writing it. Write every detail you want to see in the final image.
- Sometimes AI can read the image wrong — your job is to check or overview.

## Build a Small App

- Write with your goal in mind: how would you like the app to work (input), and how do you want the app to behave (output)?
- After getting the app, carefully review the changes and see if you need any alterations.

## Data Analysis

- When the response is without code, it may be hallucinated or guessed.
- Ask AI to "show the code" so you avoid errors or hallucination.

## Desktop Apps & Permissions

- Don't give permissions to edit all the folders, drive, or computer.
- Give permission to edit only the specific files in the folder.
- Rules to remember: Tell the ask, ask for a plan, review and edit, and only then approve.

## Which Model, When

- Some AI models are best for image generation, some for video, some for research, and some for daily tasks. Try single prompts on different models within the same AI as well as across other AIs.
